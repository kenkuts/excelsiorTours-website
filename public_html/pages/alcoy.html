Alcoy Beach

        Alcoy is a fifth class municipality in the province of Cebu. It is located in the southern part of Cebu. It is named after Alcoy which is a city of Spain. When Spaniards came to the place during the Spanish era, some of them said that the place reminded them of Spanish Alcoy, having a green and lush environment and the simplicity of the place and inhabitants. From then on, the place was called Alcoy.

        A popular beach that is located in Alcoy is called Tingko Beach, generally it is called 'Alcoy Beach.' Tingko Beach is about more than a mile long beach with pinkish and crystal clear turquoise water lined with coconut palm trees and limestone cliffs. Tingko is great for families or group guests because of its extensive beach and shallow waters that offer relaxing environment and fun activities to do. The entrance fee for this beach is only 10 pesos (roughly about 25 cents in US currency).
