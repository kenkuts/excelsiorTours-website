Malapascua Island

        Malapascua Island is a Philippine island situated in the Visayan Sea, 6.8 kilometres (4.2 mi) across a shallow strait from the northernmost tip of Cebu Island. Malapascua became famous fairly recently, only in the early 1990s as a dive destination. Prior to this, the island was known for its wide white sand beach, known as Bounty Beach; it has also become known for its beautiful coral gardens and excellent local dive spots.

        A paradise island, with beautiful bounty white beaches. The island has a big variety in landscape. All over the islands are sand roads that brings you over hills to idyllic fishing- villages, deserted bays, mountains, forest with coconut trees, but most of all serene, white beaches. These beaches are some off the most beautiful in the Philippines. Malapascua island offers a variety of places to accommodate your daily tourist needs. On the island they have shops where you can rent rooms, buy diving gear and food.
