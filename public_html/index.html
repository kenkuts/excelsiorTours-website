The Philippines

        The Philippines is an archipelagic country composed of 7,107 islands. The country's rich biodiversity is the main tourist attraction of the Philippines. Its beaches, mountains, rainforests, islands and diving spots are among the country's
most popular tourist destinations. The country's rich historical and cultural heritage is also one of the attractions of the Philippines.

        On the right you can see a map view of the Philippines and its various regions. The Excelsior Tours website will focus on tourist spots on an island called Cebu. The island of Cebu's location is in the central region of the Philippines.

Cebu

        Cebu is a long narrow island stretching 140 miles from north to south and 35 miles across at its widest point, surrounded by 167 neighbouring, smaller islands, which include Mactan, Bantayan, Malapascua, Olongapo and the Camotes Islands. Cebu is subdivided into 6 component cities and 44 municipalities. On the right you can click on the image to get a closer view of the whole map of Cebu.

        Also known as the "Queen City of the South," Cebu is one of the most developed provinces in the Philippines. With many beautiful islands, white sand beaches, luxury hotel and resorts, diving location and heritage sites, high domestic and foreign tourist arrivals have fueled the tourism industry of Cebu.
